# About Nebulo

Nebulo is a platform where we strive to list the best websites in their respective fields – websites that are **SAFE** and share our **PRINCIPLES** and **VALUES**, namely a **FREE** and **OPEN** internet for everyone.

---

## Our Mission

We firmly believe that:

- Governments should not monitor our messages
- Android should not be a locked-in platform forever
- Video games should still be playable 80 years after purchase
- DRM should not ruin the gaming experience
- Companies should not force us to log in every 30 days to renew a licence

> We are fighting for people's privacy, for people's freedom.

---

## No Paywalls, No Ad Traps

We openly call out websites that hide content behind paywalls, stuffed ads links, or aggressive monetization flows built only for profit.

For us, sharing knowledge should come first. Money should never be the reason people are blocked from information that could help them learn, build, and improve.

That is not how Nebulo works.

We do not build this platform to trap users behind ads or paid gates. We build it to share.

---

## Our Code of Ethics

We have a code of ethics that is very rare these days, but which was the norm in the early days of piracy – not to mention the C-word.

This simple code of ethics is effective; it states, for example, that a video game player should, where possible, **buy the game to support the developers**.

> Because our fight is not against the developers, but against the big companies.

Today, big companies have monopolies everywhere, which wasn’t the case before—or at least it wasn’t obvious.

---

### The Problem with Big Companies

Take the cases of **Ubisoft**, **Blizzard** and **Nintendo**, where their executives have been involved in multiple incidents of sexual assault against women, yet these cases always end without any consequences.

Companies that are absolutely determined to release a game by a deadline. I'll take the only exception that springs to mind: **Cyberpunk 2077**.

> The single best game of the last decade, which was ruined by investors who were desperate for the game to be released on 22 December before Christmas to boost sales.

The result: it was the worst video game launch of the decade. Yet, a few years later, it is undeniably the best in its category (that’s my opinion; everyone is entitled to their own).

---

### Nintendo: A Case Study

Take **Nintendo**, for example, which is releasing a Switch 2 that looks exactly the same as the Switch 1, with components that were already outdated at launch, and some absolutely disgraceful practices:

- You can no longer lend out your cartridges without risking having your console banned
- Pokémon games are just as rubbish as ever, since they're more interested in making money than putting a smile on their customers' faces

Just look at **Pokémon Legends Arceus**, where it was a major change and we needed it, and the game is as dead as my two grandparents put together, the landscape is empty, etc.

In short (we could also take the example of Pokémon Legends Z-A).

---

### The Indie Revolution

I haven't come here to air my views on the latest games released; I just want to explain that we simply want the companies to listen to us, and they've got everything to gain from it.

When you see that **Silksong** has smashed sales records on Steam – in fact, Steam actually crashed because there were so many sales – and let's not forget it's an indie game…

> That says it all.
